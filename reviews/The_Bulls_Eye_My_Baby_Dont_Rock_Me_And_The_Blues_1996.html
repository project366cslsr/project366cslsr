Raw Rockabilly with lovely sloppiness and diversity in sound and with some streams of authentic Rock'n'Roll. Two cover versions of old time originals.


"My Baby Don't Rock" is a rather crazy song, rockin' and shakin'. I liked it more than the original version. This soft rave is still stored here. But everything is made easier, in some ways faster, straightforward. Which fits this groove nicely. How cool does the intro guitar and further solos sound? So atmospheric. The second track "Me And The Blues" is a bit of classy Country Rockabilly. Very interesting tune with topnotch singing. Made without too much extra frills, but this was not required. Most of the elements show very cool casualness.


Another feature is that the team is from the 90s. On the one hand, there is no particular strangeness in this, but if you listen to the recordings without looking at the date, then it is difficult to say when it was done. So well matched for style. With its general energy and country-minded passion.
