Review

071/366 (Project 366)

Decided to make this week a bit Punkabilly, Modern Rockabilly and Psychobilly flavoured one. So, this band - Rocker Arms - are Rock'n'Roll with a Punk-sound influence, mad, psycho and fun themes. As a result, their sound is diverse from release to release, from song to song - but, in general, possible to call it as Punkabilly, modern Rockabilly with soft psychopop around. Some of their albums are full of buzzin' drive, trashy force and rockin' roll. Practically always there are might of general Rock, a modish modern Rockabilly tone and punky garage craze.


A kind of MiniAlbum, "Leaving Jersey Shore", begins with a fancy story about "Joey". Very lethal tune, increasingly growing speed and beat, energetic, raging vibe. The next song "Closer to Me" with effects and feelings of seething boiling, and a kind of warming up with a funny transition to chant! Beautiful melodies, rocking spices, backing vocals, solos and unforgiving rhythm! The third song, "Free Beer 4 All", is a quite fairy song, starts a bit scary and misty - but then it switched to impulse and smashed all things around. It trembles wonderfully. Then "Don't Tell Me" is a rather punky hardcore song. So straight-out, so clear. Thoroughgoing snorting and knockdown. Tough, solid, radical. Then a very mysterious melody with the fifth track "Dressed In Red", even a little surfing.. horror surfin' tune. Unhurried but very formidable and doomy. Exciting before the final splash, furious! And, actually, epic. Which continues very much with the next instrumental "Punk No. 1" - amazing blend of things. Back to the modern and oldschool. Mighty and rockin'. A kind of mini-album ends with a curious song "28". Rockin' and rollin' chic end. "Twenty Eight - my Lucky Numbers".. really love it. So straight and genuinely.


Totally, you can note fast speed, strong rhythm, really good vocals (for style), charm of double bass, energy of psycho blast and a really rockin' punky groove. And I think these songs are more about "punkabilly", than about blend of Punk-Rock music with Psychobilly or modern Rockabilly or alternative Rock'n'Roll. At least, by its its composition, its drive and spirit. And perhaps a very smooth punkabilly shape without noticeably strong skewing on one side. And the relative impossibility of declaring belonging to a particular style (as something purely distinctive).
