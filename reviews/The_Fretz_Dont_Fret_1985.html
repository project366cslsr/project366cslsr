Review

247/366 (Project 366)

Eighties Psychobilly and (neo)Rockabilly Rock'n'Roll outfit. May remind me of Fractured and (uh) the fiercest and wildest sides of the Jets the same years as the release. But, most likely, the Fretz was inspired by other things and the scene in general. And the songs themselves, the sound, the recording are quite distinctive and original.


The first track "Don't Fret" is a groovy rockin' and rollin' Rockabilly Revival with influence of so called (neo)Rockabilly and softened Psychobilly. Lively enthusiasm, very stylistically oriented arrangements. Assertive but melodic. Then really catching second track "Better Change Your Ways" with a flyin' manic tune, somewhat misty and shady beginning and then a psycho raving tone. Quite fast, grinding, madness, drive with a choppy, rip and toss, whip and rocking mood! The third song "Wishful Thinking", like the previous tracks, is highly charged by the double bass. And, maybe, I could point out that this track is a bit unified in the style of the previous two, but with slightly more poppy and fifties inspired. Still a little nervous tune, crazy groove and style.The fourth song, "A Place In The Sun", is a conceptual rock action with echoes, pushy leaks and riffs. The song even contains a very cool piece and shouting. Hard rockin' and rollin' psychos.


I think this is a rather great example of UK Rockabilly and its (neo) and alternative sides, very tuneful singing and this is actually a pearl EP. Decently atmospheric, a little goofy in the mood, with pertinent backing vocals, and just smooth but a craze!
