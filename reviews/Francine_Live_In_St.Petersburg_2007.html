Review

Famous band from Finland with live gig at St.Petersburg, Russia! Modern Rockabilly with elements of soft Psychobilly and full of Rock'n'Roll.


They are Francine... so, smooth and dashing start with a great song "Strychnine". Really feels the best of their style! Simply heavy rockin' and rollin' with the sweetest Modern Rockabilly sound, charismatic vocals and catchy chanting. Shaking with further song "Whatcha Gonna Do" - smooth switch, nice roll. Pretty straightforward composition, but very atmospheric flavored with vocals. It sounds very epic. The third song "Freakshow" is presented as a fresh composition at the time. The track begins with a bit creepy laugh after introduction and continues with hard rockin' sound - modishly. In general, the song reminds and creates a lot of sensations. Pretty dynamic tune. Then wonderful track for the "Annie". Uh... melodiously, lovely and even still rough! Song with an interesting motive and stage excitement.

Another fresh track of that time was presented as from the upcoming album. And titled "King For A Day". A rather uncompromising tune, with an assertive sound, flawless lyrics. All this is even a bit of alternative rock with straight up thrum. The drums create a special mood, guitar solos make your wonder a bit, then vocals back you to heat! A cool track for an excited and kindly crazy crowd. The next song (already the sixth) "That Little One" is sounds as a hit. Tender motive, kind start and rockin' continues. Modern Rock'n'Roll with backtune of vintage sweets. Affably performing and still in a rather unique style of Francine. Balanced between a pretty soft and really heavy tune. Great example is a completely furious composition "Talking Heads". Coolest drive! Picturesque and even probably in the best traditions of the late eighties old school and soft psychobilly. This is the case even for the next hard'n'heavy track "Flames Of Hell". And even more this is the case for the "She's Dynamite" - speed, furious and migthy track. For me it sounds as an indicative sound of Modern Rockabilly. Influence of general Rock'n'Roll, alternative rock and dynamic rave tune. The tenth track "You Get Me Wrong" is one more Rock'n'Roll blast, suitable for a wide range of listeners. Flying start of "Never Let You Down" is as cool and great as the track itself. Flows, streams and waves of chicly chosen arrangement. The next song "She Bop", like the "She's Dynamite" too, is very expressive, powerful and energetic. Furious Roll and a pretty good snack before the final song of the show - slightly swampy roll "Good Bye Forever". Boppin', rockin', rollin' and even small sing-a-long from crowd at the end. And, of course, We Want More! However, this Live album is over. But really want more.


I like how the tracks flow incredibly smoothly, keeps the feeling of a live performance.
