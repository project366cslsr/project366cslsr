Review

093/366 (Project 366)

The first of April ended, but a kind of silly and even crazy mood remained around. So, I decided to review something looney, but with a good sense of it. Hawkeye and Hoe are from Denmark with pretty conceptual music that is ranged but around rusty, country, scrumpy, cow rockin', hillbilly, folk, somewhat rockabilly and so things. Mostly fast, reckless and furiously rustic Country music. However, with the visible influence of stylish Folk and Hillbilly music. But most importantly - some comedy art and cheerful entertainment.


The first track "Molly Let Yer Bangs Hang Down" is straight filled with such a rather artistic manner of performance, sparkling and cheerful. Pretty funny. Next song "Gentleman Soldier", maybe, with a touch of shanties. The track also has a variation and a sketch about the famous "Soldier, soldier won't you marry me". Third track "Ole Slewfoot" with their clear style. Banjos, guitars and other attributes of rustic country rave. This tune also reminds me of "One Woman Man" a bit, by its so lovely imagination voice sound. Catchy track "Nobodies Business" with furious solos on banjo and sounds nice itself. Cover song "Setting the Woods Onfire" with a smooth and quite raw style. Still recognizable. Still special.

Sixth —Åomposition "Shimme" is all about barn rave'n'roll, with a nice arrangement and craziest pieces of theme. Loony track! The next song "Long Journey Home", maybe, pretented on band's side of old time twistin' Hillbilly. Country song with a popular theme. The eighth song "Working in a Coalmine" is very ambitious. Rockin' country bumpkins with a non-stop pump process (well, working... in a coalmine). The next track "Railroad, Steamboat" in a relative manner. With quite houseparty start, then unified concept of all the tracks can be heard there. The tenth song "I Am a Cider Drinker" is a uncouth cover of English band's (the Wurzels) hit. I like that song, I enjoyed with this cover version!


The band performs emotionally, brightly, fervently and with tireless enthusiasm. Slightly raucous vocals, loud instrumental sound and pumping tunes. Real cow rockin' in one gulp. Rustic set of musical instruments makes the sound especially entourage.
