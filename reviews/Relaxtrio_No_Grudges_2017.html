Review

107/366 (Project 366)

A very groovy and bright start to the song "Blackguard Johnny" in the vein of Modern Rockabilly. And then a very handsome female vocal comes in, then potent chorus with male backing vocals. What a wonder tune! Actually, simple and catchy. Also the guitar sounds skilfully awesome, doublebass with clear sound and drums.. as drums should be. The second track "Please Just Leave Suzie" is more quiet and a little more nifty. With a mischievous female voice, rough and cool sound. Still wonderful guitar accompaniment and confident double bass. The song has a small pause at the end and a powerful ending in the context of the song. Third track is a furious radio edited "Don't You Mess with Me" - absolutely rockin' shake! Wild and a bit crazy tune. But so lovely and lively. The EP closes with a very pretty stylish sounding song. Modern Country rocking tune. And, basically, good modern Rock'n'Roll. In general, songs with a rather sultry tone. Although a little overloaded and heavy, they sound very balanced and pleasant. For this kind of music. Actually, each of their new releases brings more and more their own unique style, more mature sound and variety in arrangements, as well as general mood. And, in general, becoming a little tougher and heavy enough for being around modern rockin' and rollin' psychobilly bands.


Quite an interesting and original band with heavy but smooth sound. The influence of somewhat Hard Rock, Heavy and Alternative Rock is felt. They seem to be quite enthusiastic musicians who can play indescribably fast and speedy when necessary and do pretty melodic things when required. Might, enchantingly powerful vocals, fiercely in lyrics and sound. Well, they make a variety of tunes with consistent quality performance. And on their own way.
