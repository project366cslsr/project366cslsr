Review

010/366 (Project 366)

Pretty and lovely schlager rockabilly sound. With marvelous female vocal that lead distinctly classic rockabilly tune shining and sparkling. All is focused on instruments. Doublebass, drums, guitar and voice are there! It is melodious and wisdomly. Vocal with, perhaps, jaunty manner of performance. That is quite suitable for a tribute to Johnny Burnette.


I think that a vital part of the interest in the band is their frontwoman. This is quite logical and understandable. A charming woman in music is always something attractive and special. When the voice sounds enticing and other musicians can provide cool support - then hits appear. Old School Cats gains and share many features from winsome lady. Since, yes, all of these songs are well known. But here they wearing makeup. In their own way. How a woman does. And it turns out quite distinctive peculiarity. With girlish ardor and slightly soaring artistry. First review of Project366 was with connection to Eddie Cochran. Thus, here is choice for Johnny Burnette.


Lonesome Train - ahw! stylish... nifty.. bogey. There you really start to think that Lady Sings Burnette. Yeah, songs that are Lady sung! All things that many loved with Burnette look - find there with an amazing lady look.. lady look.. but not least burnetteable than original chic!


Good to hear sweet "You're Sixteen" with this cherry voice! Dreamy dreamy. Cute cute... guitar is pretty atmospheric. Doublebass is unified with drums on it rhythmbuilding. Backing vocals are so atmospheric. And so beautiful vocals just become so brightful.


Well-known hit for cats. Uhm... wild wild tune. Rockabilly Boogie as is! Teddy?! Yeah! Too catchy version. Not so crazy, but pretty wild and calmly twirls swirl-a-whirl. Chopping chic! Gurgling guitars. Stalwart delight.


All By Myself still with its pretty quite wild arrangements what is pretty for soft boppin' act. Backings there are a bit fancy (but voice is still perfect and honeyed). Schlager, pop, rockabilly, bop!


Songs are pretty melodic, rockin' and rollin' with shadows of bop and vintage. Despite many sparkles - it is still quite good rockabilly. No-frills, but interesting.
